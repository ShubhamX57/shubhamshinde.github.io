01 Background & skills

From mechanical engineering
to research software.

I trained first as a mechanical engineer in Pune, India, and discovered partway through simulating wind-turbine blades in CFD that the problems I cared most about were the computational ones. That pulled me toward a PG Diploma in Data Science & AI, award-winning research in aerial image analysis, and now an MSc at the University of Bristol, funded by the competitive SARTHI National Scholarship.

Three years across a defence aerodynamics R&D laboratory, an ML startup, and clinical imaging taught me what production software actually demands: thorough testing, reproducible environments, and systems a colleague can understand and operate without me in the room.

My current research is in quasi-elastic neutron scattering (QENS), where I'm building open-source analysis tools that follow the conventions used at large-scale facilities such as the ISIS Neutron Source. The appeal is the combination — physics models that live or die against experimental data, and the engineering discipline of making that analysis fast, correct, and shareable.

Outside the work itself, I serve as the academic representative for my MSc cohort and maintain digital platforms for two professional engineering societies. I remain quietly convinced that a good README is an act of kindness.

QENS Analysis & Visualisation Suite
MSc capstone · University of Bristol · methodology aligned with ISIS Neutron Source workflows
2025 – present

An open-source pipeline for quasi-elastic neutron scattering (QENS) analysis — my MSc capstone. It implements the full physics chain from raw HDF5 files through resolution correction, model fitting, and Bayesian parameter estimation, surfaced through a FastAPI + React interface.

The core model is the Chudley–Elliott jump-diffusion framework, describing how molecules hop between sites in a liquid or confined geometry. Getting the resolution-function deconvolution right was the decisive challenge — a naive approach inflates the diffusion coefficient by roughly 20%.

Chudley–Elliott structure factor
$$S(Q,\omega) = \frac{1}{\pi}\cdot\frac{\Gamma(Q)}{\omega^2 + \Gamma^2(Q)}$$
where $\Gamma(Q)=\dfrac{D\,Q^2}{1+Q^2 l^2}$ — $D$ = diffusion coefficient, $l$ = jump length
Resolution-corrected measured spectrum
$$I(Q,\omega) = S(Q,\omega)\otimes R(Q,\omega) + B(Q)$$
$R(Q,\omega)$ = instrumental resolution · $B(Q)$ = background
Open source repository
github.com/ShubhamX57/QENS
Key results
  • ~16% reduction in systematic error on the diffusion coefficient via physics-correct resolution correction
  • 3× better χ² goodness-of-fit for Chudley–Elliott vs a Fickian diffusion model
  • Bayesian MCMC across 4 independent chains, convergence verified at Gelman–Rubin $\hat{R}<1.01$
  • FastAPI + React interface with planned cloud deployment
